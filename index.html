Progress Update

Material procurement

  • We have successfully acquired all necessary materials for the project, including the swimming ring, Fresnel lens, and light absorbing materials. .
  • The swimming ring, as the outer layer of the whole solar evaporation system, provides buoyancy to the whole system so that it can float on the water surface.
  • The Fresnel lens, as the key device of our whole system, effectively gathers the sunlight. It has an outer diameter of 30cm and a focal length of 40cm.
  • . For the core device of the system, the solar interface evaporator light absorbing module, we used black polyurethane foam. It has high light absorption efficiency, effectively absorbs solar radiation and converts it into heat energy.

Experimental simulation

  • Basic experiments to simulate the process have been conducted. We have done a simulation experiment with equal scale reduction.

Problems encountered:

  • In the course of the experiment, we discovered that the melting point of polyurethane material is relatively low. When it is exposed to high temperatures, especially after using the Fresnel lens to concentrate light, the polyurethane degrades and even burns under concentrated solar radiation. This limits its suitability for high temperature solar thermal applications. We needed to consider replacing the material.
  • In the very first system design, we considered using a special black hydrogel as a light absorbing material. But considering that it is not easy to obtain and complicated to fabricate, we replaced it with a more readily available polyurethane sponge. But the experimental results were unsatisfactory, and we considered using it back to the original design idea.
